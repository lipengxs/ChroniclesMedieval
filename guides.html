2026 Battle Systems Quick Guide

Plan Before Contact

Recent developer updates describe battles as a sequence that begins before the first charge. Use deployment zones, battle lines, standing orders, and initial orders to shape how your army behaves once combat starts.

  • Put ranged units on flanks or clear sight lines
  • Keep defensive infantry where the enemy must commit
  • Assign initial orders for cavalry sweeps or ridge control

Use Formations Deliberately

Base formations keep units mobile; advanced formations are powerful but situational. Switch only when contact is close enough to justify the reformation time.

  • Line and loose formations help movement and missile survival
  • Shield wall and spear wall hold or punish charges
  • Wedge and skein formations are for decisive breaks

Protect Morale

Morale is described as a core battle system, with units moving through inspired, confident, concerned, wavering, and broken states. Your commander can lift morale through visible heroics, but falling in battle can damage the whole army.

  • Watch flanks because flank pressure can break confidence
  • Retreat preserves forces better than a rout
  • Use command mode when the whole army needs redirection

Political Sandbox Priorities

Track Loyalty Before War

Recent developer diaries frame battles as outcomes of lordship, loyalty, rivalries, and political pressure. Watch which vassals benefit from your decisions before committing to a campaign.

  • Do not assume every ally will stay loyal indefinitely
  • Use smaller conflicts to test army reliability
  • Keep reserves for political surprises after a battle

Fight for Useful Ground

Battlefields are shaped around terrain and army scale. Choose fights where your army's culture and unit mix can use the landscape rather than forcing every battle into a frontal push.

  • Archers want clear sight lines and flanks
  • Cavalry needs room to commit before impact
  • Infantry anchors work best near terrain blockers

Win the Aftermath

Victory and defeat do not end the campaign. Preserve elite units, avoid unnecessary routs, and think about what your weakened neighbors or rivals will do next.

  • Retreat early if the strategic goal is already lost
  • Protect your commander from morale collapse
  • Use post-battle momentum to resolve rival claims
